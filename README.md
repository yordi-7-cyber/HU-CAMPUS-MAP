## 🎓 About Haramaya University

Haramaya University (HU) is one of Ethiopia's oldest and most prestigious public universities, originally founded in **1954** as the Imperial College of Agricultural and Mechanical Arts with assistance from Oklahoma State University. Located in **Haramaya, East Hararghe Zone, Oromia Region**, it sits about **510 km east of Addis Ababa** and **14 km northwest of Harar city**.

Today HU is a comprehensive research university with:
- **32,000+ students** (2023)
- **1,600+ academic staff**
- **78 undergraduate**, **110 masters**, and **28 PhD programs**
- **9 colleges**, **2 institutes**, **1 academy**, and multiple directorates
- One of the **largest agricultural experiment stations in Africa** (350+ hectares main campus)

### 2. Harar Health Campus (College of Health and Medical Sciences — CHMS)
Located in **Harar town**, approximately **23 km from the main campus**. Established in **September 1996**, the Health Campus moved to Harar in **October 2003** to give students direct access to nearby hospitals for clinical training.

**Schools within CHMS:**
- School of Medicine
- School of Medical Laboratory Sciences
- School of Nursing and Midwifery
- School of Pharmacy
- Department of Public Health
- Department of Environmental Health Sciences

**Current enrollment:** ~1,770 undergraduate + 593 masters + 120 residency + 53 PhD students

**Facilities at Harar Campus:**
- Laboratories (clinical, microbiology, anatomy)
- Campus library
- Cafeteria
- Dormitories (male and female blocks)
- Recreational rooms
- Kersa Demographic Surveillance and Health Research Center

**Affiliated hospitals for clinical training:**
- Hiwot Fana Specialized University Hospital (Harar)
- Jugal Hospital (Harar)
- Dilchora Referral Hospital (Dire Dawa)

---

### 3. Haramaya Institute of Technology (HiT) Campus
The engineering and technology campus, home to the **Haramaya Institute of Technology (HiT)**. Focuses on applied sciences and engineering disciplines. Located adjacent to or near the main campus area.

**Programs include:**
- Civil Engineering
- Electrical & Computer Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering
- Chemical Engineering
- Software Engineering
- Architecture

---

### 4. Station Campus
The agricultural research and experimental station campus. Houses the university's massive **agricultural experiment station** — one of the largest in Africa. Used primarily for research, field experiments, livestock management, and postgraduate agricultural studies.

### Getting to Addis Ababa from Harar/Haramaya

Buses depart **daily at 04:30–05:00** from Harar bus station. Buy tickets **2–3 days in advance**. Major companies: Selam Bus, Habesha Bus. Journey takes **~11 hours**. Arrives at Meskel Square, Addis Ababa.

> **Tip for freshmen:** The university sometimes arranges orientation-week transport. Check with the Freshman Program Directorate on arrival.

## 🌊 Lakes & Natural Attractions

### Lake Haramaya (Lake Alemaya)
Historically one of the most important lakes in eastern Ethiopia, located **14 km northwest of Harar** and right next to the university. The lake has faced severe environmental degradation over recent decades due to overuse and climate change — much of it dried up in the early 2000s. However, **restoration efforts are ongoing** and parts of the lake are being revived.

- Historically used for irrigation, fishing, and recreation
- A key research subject for HU's environmental science programs
- Scenic walking area around the lake bed and restored wetlands
- Birdwatching opportunities (migratory birds)

### Lake Adele (Adele Wetlands)
A smaller wetland area near the campus, important for biodiversity and used in environmental research.

## 🏙️ Tourism — Harar & Surroundings

Harar is a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** — the ancient walled city (Jugol) is one of the most historically significant places in the Horn of Africa and is considered the **fourth holiest city of Islam** (after Mecca, Medina, and Jerusalem), with **82 mosques** and **102 shrines**.

### Must-See in Harar

| Attraction | Details |
|---|---|
| Harar Jugol (Old Walled City) | UNESCO site, maze of alleys, colorful houses, 5 historic gates |
| Hyena Feeding | Nightly tradition — feed wild hyenas by hand. ~500 ETB for tourists |
| Rimbaud's House & Museum | Former home of French poet Arthur Rimbaud, now a museum |
| Feres Magala Square | Central square of the old city |
| Harar Spice & Coffee Market | Incredible variety of Harar coffee (Geographical Indication protected) and spices |
| Gidir Magala (Main Market) | Traditional cloth, food, and goods market |
| Harar Brewery | Home of the famous Harar Beer |
| Khat Market (Aweday) | Largest khat market in Ethiopia, ~10 km west of Harar |
| Babile Camel Market | Large weekly camel market, ~50 km east of Harar |
| Valley of Marvels (Dakhata Valley) | Spectacular rock formations beyond Babile |
| Babile Elephant Sanctuary | One of the few elephant sanctuaries in eastern Ethiopia |
| Ejersa Goro | Birthplace of Emperor Haile Selassie, ~30 km from Harar |
